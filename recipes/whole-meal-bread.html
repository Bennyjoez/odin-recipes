Whole Meal Bread Recipe

Description

Wholemeal bread is a type of bread made using flour that is partly or entirely milled from whole or almost-whole wheat grains. Baking the bread takes up 30 to 40 minutes at a temperature of 200 to 260°C.

Ingredients

  • 300 grams white bread flour or all-purpose flour
  • 200 grams whole-wheat flour or whole-grain spelt flour
  • 7 grams (1 packet) active dry yeast or instant yeast
  • 10 grams brown sugar or maple syrup
  • 10 grams kosher salt
  • 400 grams (1⅔ cups) warm water
  • 30 grams mixed seeds such as sunflower, sesame, pumpkin, or flaxseeds (optional)
  • 10 grams old-fashioned rolled oats (optional)

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, whisk the bread flour, whole-wheat flour, yeast, sugar, and salt until combined.
  2. Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients and add the water.
  3. Using a wooden spoon, stir the mixture until no dry spots remain and you have a uniform, loose, and slightly sticky dough.
  4. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or a damp cloth, and let the dough rise in a warm place until it doubles in size, about 1 hour.
  5. Lightly grease a 9-inch loaf pan with vegetable oil or cooking spray and line it with a piece of parchment paper, leaving a bit of overhang on each side to easily transfer the loaf once baked.
  6. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit.
  7. Transfer the risen bread dough to a clean, floured surface, and fold the edges of the dough over the center to form a rectangular shape.
  8. Transfer the dough, seam-side down, to the prepared loaf tin.
  9. In a small mixing bowl, mix the seeds and oats, if using.
  10. Generously coat the top of the loaf with the seed and oat mixture.
  11. Let the dough proof in the tin for 15 minutes.
  12. After proofing is complete, bake the loaf until it is golden brown and the internal temperature reaches 140 degrees Fahrenheit, about 50–55 minutes.
  13. Remove the loaf from the oven and let it cool for 5 minutes before lifting it out of the tray and transferring it to a wire rack.
  14. Let the loaf cool to room temperature before slicing, at least 30 minutes.
